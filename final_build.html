Mechanism

The mechanism is a form of mechanical actuator as it turns circular motion into linear motion. This can be seen as a motor creates circular movement and this opens the jaw and moves the tongue forwards and backwards. (ElProCus, n.d.)

When a hand is sensed and then moved it away, the jaw would open and the tongue would move out. This would then pump soap onto the user’s hand. The soap would then continue to come out of the tongue until the user moved their hand away. At this point the tongue would retract and the jaw would close. This educes the chance of the user getting their hand trapped within the jaw as it opens and closes, while also allowing them to choose how much soap they would like to use.

Base

On the 14th May the final prototype of the base was made; it was much more suited for its purpose. The size of the stand was significantly reduced so it was more likely to fit behind the skinks in a bathroom. The door for accessing soap storage was moved onto the front of the base. This was done so that staff could conveniently swap soap without needing to move anything. If it was at the back of the base, the entire dispenser and base would need to be lifted to swap soap, and this could become problematic due to the weight and size.

When exploring materials to build the base many options were considered. The final prototype was built out of wood, this was because it was a cheaper option to use for a final prototype, it was also sturdy and provide a clear demonstration of what the final product would look like. If wood was to be used for a final product it needs to be oiled to make it waterproof. Even though wood oils can be found that create a waterproof, oil is susceptible to wear and tear which will likely happen with a soap dispenser in a commercial bathroom. Therefore, an option to consider would be with acrylic which would be laser cut to size.

Although this may be a costly option it would create a completely waterproof solution that would have a cleaner better finished look than wood. On top of this, laser cutting is an accurate option, meaning the same item can be reproduced time and time again. This is because a file can be uploaded into a machine and replicated for hours For both of these options, the recycling solution is for them to be sent to specific plants which handle the recycling. Although once at the plants the recycling is completed in a different manor, for the company purchasing the artefact, the situation would be the same. (Martínez-Conde, A, et al, 2017; KEYi Tech, 2025; xTool, 2025; Recycle Now, n.d.)

Combine Everything

Every stage needed minor or major adjustments for a final prototype to be completed. To start with, the base would need keyhole mounts to be added. Given that the base is made out of wood this would be created using a Dremel. A hole would also need to be drilled in the top of the storage to allow for a tube to be fed through and into the throat section the Soapomorph to allow for the movement of soap. If the base was to be laser cut these would be considered within the design.

The artefact needed to fully printed and assembled. It needed the electronics to be placed inside that would work the motor for the mechanism. The Mechanism would also need to be implemented so that the jaw could open and the tongue could come out.

Once these parts were done a tube would be passed from the soap holder, through the base, through the throat parts and finally through the tongue with an opening at the end of the togue. It was also need a system to pump the soap from the dispenser all the way to the end of the mouth. This then would create a completed prototype, ignoring ideal finishes and material choices.
