Initial Concept

When the task was presented to the group, multiple ideas were brainstormed. This includes:
• Windmill
• Drone
• Band
• Octopus
• Butterfly
• Dinosaur
• Xenomorph from alien

From this point it was very quickly decided that the focus was directed at the Xenomorph from the Alien films. Given the shape of the tongue the group decided it would be interesting to create a soap dispenser, with the tongue dispensing the soap. As can be seen below, original ideas had a retractable tongue, electronics stored in the top parts of its head and a pump in order to pump soap from where it was stored. To resolve potential copyright problems, the name of the artefact was labelled “Soapomorph”. (IMDb, 1979)

Also ideas of how it was going to mounted were discussed. Although not decided fully at this original stage, whether it was going to be mounted or put onto a base was considered. One of the original sketches for how it would be mounted can be seen below, this is a key hole moutning bracket (what they actually look like is also displayed).

Also part of the discussion was how the tongue would extend in order to dispense soap. The main concern was a safety issue as finger entrapent was a possibilty if the tongue retracted while the use still had their hand collecting soap. On top of this how the artefact was going to deteriorate over time needed to be considered for a number if reasons. To start, if specfic parts broken, like the track for the tongue it could risk injuring a user. But a second reason is, it would be more ideal for a company to only replace specific parts rather than a whole model in order for them to save money.

Artefact Design

Once the idea was defined some 2d images were created to build off of. These had a general idea of where things would be positioned, how parts would be attached to each other and a general size guide. These 2D designs focused more so on the skull as this was going to be the most important section of the project given that it planned to hold everything from the electronics to the soap. The original design suggested that the top of the skull would be attached by magnets this would have been ideal for accessing the electronics. It also suggested that the soap would be placed inside of the skull as well as can be suggested by the tubes feeding into the tongue. Although measurements were added, the tongue was not to scale.

On the 2nd march 2026, a skull was developed on Blender, this was 3D modeled as the original aim was to not print it but to demonstrate an idea. This was done by having a mesh cube that was expanded to match the shape of an overlayed human skull. The back of the skull was given more detail through the use of loop cuts and extruding vertices and faces to create a more sci-fi image. Teeth were also added to this model however, due to some difficulty attachting them to the skull, it was decided that these would be a seperate model. However, due to the design being hand modelled there were multiple issues with proportions and some underlying geometry issues. On top of this when autosmoothing was completed on the skull it removed the intimidating alien look that was wanted.

As a result of this on the 16th March 2026, a skull ornament was taken to the 3D scanning department and scanned into the system. This created a design that could be developed from to create a final prototype. This method allowed for a more accurate design than the original 3D model. This creates a better foundation for more aspects of the design to be built of it. However, something to consider when 3D scanning is that it struggles to scan reflective materials.

Blender has a General Public license meaning it is not only free to use but they also ensure that any artwork created on blender is the artists property. However, if a company was to use Fusion for manufacturing it would cost approximately £1842 per year. This is a significant cost difference (Blender Foundation, n.d.; Autodesk, 2026)

On the 17th March a prototype of the inner jaw was made. This again was a 3D model as it was developed to highlight a general idea to the group rather than create a prototype to be printed. Also on this date, fangs were modelled to add to the 3D scanned skull. The skull was also flattened at the back because it needed to be extended to better resemble a Xenomorph skull.

On the 28th March 2026 the design was improved to make the model more Xenomorph like. The side of the head was moulded so the side and back of the head was more xenomorph like.

On the 16th April 2026 the tongue section was modelled. This started as a cube which was re sized and edited to resemble the xenomorph tongue. Once a general shape had been made parts were bevelled in order to create an uneven surface again to resemble the xenomorph tongue. It is also important to note that this had a central hole to allow for the tube to flow through it. This was because the soap would come out of the tongue when used.

Also, on this day the jaw was formed. Similar to the original 3D modelling this was completed by having a human jaw and editing a cube to fit into the shape. Once a basic jaw model had been created it was designed to match the theming of the project with fangs being added as the final step.

Base Design

Once a developed idea of the artefact had been created, how it was going to be held needed to be assessed. This was a lengthy discussion as some group members thought it would be best to mount it on a back panel, and some believed it would be better to be mounted onto a stand. A combination of these methods was implemented.

The original drawings for the base were completed on the 15th April 2026. They suggested quite a large base area that would be able to store the soap that the dispenser was using. It would also be big enough to store spare soap when needed. However, the issue with this was that it was too large to realistically sit on a sink.

As a result of the size problem, multiple options were considered. The option to turn it into an entire sink was discussed. As you can see below, some theme parks have hand wash stations spread across the park. However, this would mean implementing a soap dispenser and a plumbing system to allow it to be a complete system. Overall, this system was deemed not to be the best option.
